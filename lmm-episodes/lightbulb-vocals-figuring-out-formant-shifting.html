hostNote
Formant is just mouth shape, the shape of the cavity through which sound travels. That's the whole definition. But shifting those resonance points was a lightbulb moment for me as a vocalist, because it means your voice isn't fixed. You've probably already done it in everyday life without noticing. I trace formant shifting from the talk box and vocoder to how it defines a huge swath of current music, it's in the production you're hearing everywhere right now, and it's not going anywhere because at its core it's still the human voice. The episode covers vocal placement, what "presence" actually means acoustically, and how to hear where in the body a singer is placing their sound. You finish with a clearer ear for what you're hearing in modern records, and a framework for experimenting with your own voice beyond what you think your natural register is.
