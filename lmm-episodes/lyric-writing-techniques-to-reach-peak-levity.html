    hostNote
    The verse isn't landing, and you know it. The question is whether you have a move. Internal vs. external perspective is one of the most practical levers I know, the difference between "I made too much coffee because that's what I was used to" and a lyric that stays behind the eyes versus one that walks you through a scene you can see. I draw from what I learned from mentor Andrea Stolpy and Pat Pattinson on structured free writing: generating a pile of fragments, then finding the lines inside them. Vignettes, sensory detail, flipping who's talking, these aren't tricks, they're the workflow. The goal is to make decisions quickly and write right on both counts. What you take away is a repeatable process for getting unstuck mid-session, regardless of genre.
