Content

Track five on Big Hug, featuring Malik LovesYall (the same collaborator who co-wrote All in the Feeling). Luke wrote, performed, and mixed; October 2021, also released as a standalone single. It's the chill counterweight to the LP's anxiety satire: less Don't Worry breakdown, more domestic-bliss fantasy. The hook stacks the pun, kick it in pajamas and getting wavy with that good grass on the way to kick it in nirvana, bliss state and band-name joke at once. Same SoCal weed thread as I Live in California, but romantic instead of rent satire.

The first half is room-bound love, waking up at noon and probably never even really left the room except for sun and breeze, with care offered as cooking rather than hustle. The middle plays stoned phone etiquette for a laugh: Mother Mary present / can't be all wilding when I talk on the phone, the sacred name treated as a roommate you have to whisper around. Malik's verse goes cosmic-athletic, body-and-nature meditation that lands on an adobe-colored goddess and a utopian close, grow vegetables while we tend the seas... nourish many nations with our legacy. Garden as world-building, Big Hug softness at scale.

Pairs with Heffalumps and Woozles and All in the Feeling as the LP's collaborator stretch, the first record where guests enter without breaking the Scoobert shell (Big Hug essay).
