Content

B-side to #Grownupz on FEiN Times (Issue #1) (August 25, 2014): track two on the two-song single, 3:18 (SoundExchange). SoundCloud. Walton/Woodward co-write. Not on Little Homes (2016); the A-side later became track five on the LP and the viral single.

The inaugural FEiN Times zine shipped with a handmade copy of the music and a Recording Journal naming money as Issue #1's theme. Walton and Woodward introduced their first two songs as Monay Grabba and #Grownupz (they like to spell things wrong): Monay Grabba explores **imperialism, Manifest Destiny, and bad missionaries; #Grownupz is the flip, a pill-poppin, jump-jumpin trust-fund baby that doesn't wanna grow up anyway. The pair were written in practice rooms, apartments in Venice and Downtown LA, walks to Which Wich, a little home in Westlake, and voice notes in traffic, between Rick and Morty, Death Note, and word-play jokes. Drum tracking for what became Little Homes* began at LMU with engineer Michael Christofi; mobile sessions and Frank Rosato's mix followed.

Music Connection: Music Connection covered FEiN Times (Issue #1) twice in the Issue #1 launch window. Sep 26, 2014 FEiN Facebook (archivist proof: /evidence/fein-music-connection-artist-of-week-sep-2014.png): We're the Music Connection Magazine new artist of the week!! — NMC Artist of the Week on musicconnection.com: 8.0 total (Production · Lyrics · Music · Vocals · Musicianship all 8); Alternative · seeking Label, Film/TV · FEiN@FEintimes.com · feintimes.com; review names #Grownupz and Money Grabba, dazzling electro production touches, alt-rock with ADD-like energy. Jan 11, 2015 (archivist proof: /evidence/fein-music-connection-top-25-2014.png): Top 25 New Music Critiques for 2014 (Special Year End Issue — also Hot 100 Live Unsigned Artists & Bands) — same FEiN … 8.0 clipping; FEiN noted USC-alumni acts in the same issue (Huxlee, Mora Mora, Kiven, Sex Helicopter, dale).

On the lyric: a believer who can't do wrong, cloaked in culture so thoroughly he doesn't know he's alone. Money grabber, penny snatcher, dirty hands, everything you are they'll adore. Same FEiN wealth satire as #Grownupz but aimed at missionary imperialism rather than inheritance. Pairs with American Man and Lonely People as early FEiN Times social critique.
