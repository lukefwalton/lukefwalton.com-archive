Track five on [*Finding $D*](/albums/finding-d-remastered/), Luke solo; [every song on the LP](/albums/finding-d-remastered/) was written, recorded, mixed, and mastered in a single day. It sits between [*My Meddling Kids (Hallelujah Sexy)*](/songs/my-meddling-kids-hallelujah-sexy/) and [*Economic Kama Sutra*](/songs/economic-kama-sutra/), and it treats **anxiety as religion**.

The intro calls for help in three directions, mother, brother, and a *hey oh Jesus, stop* where *stop* is both prayer and plea. Then the pre-chorus names the real deity: anxiety, addressed as *my oldest love, OG*, the longest relationship in the room.

From there the chorus takes the idiom and runs it until it breaks (*damned if you do if you did if you did*), with no exit branch left. A brief riot offers collective violence as a release valve before the loop closes back over it. The ending says *afraid of missing out* twice: FOMO as the decision the song never manages to make. Same dread register as [*My Friend, Scoobert*](/songs/my-friend-scoobert/) and, later, [*Take a Breath*](/songs/take-a-breath/) (body panic on *Little Hug*), except the OG here is anxiety, not COVID.
