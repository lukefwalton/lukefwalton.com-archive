Track four on [*Little Hug*](/albums/little-hug/) (**April 2021**). The title is spelled **Premadonna** on the EP; the lyrics say *prima donna*. Luke solo. Class satire on the small recovery record, before *Big Hug*'s crypto/MLM stack but running the same American math: parents, paychecks, bodies, debt.

The premise is a refusal mistaken for vanity: *calling me a prima donna / just because I never wanna beg for a dollar*. The alternatives are escape fantasies (sauna, Tijuana, *that's what a free man do*), the same *kick it* energy as later [*Kick It in Nirvana*](/songs/kick-it-in-nirvana/), here broke and bitter. The confusion is inherited honestly: a kid who never understood why his parents went grim over *brightly colored envelops*, or why his father was miserable when *ticker tape was drowning in the red*.

The chorus is where it turns sharp. Debt collection becomes state violence (*they'll send their dogs and drag your ass on down*), and healthcare becomes mutual extraction: *the doctor picked our pocket / got his student loan to pay*. Patient and physician trapped in the same machine, an idea [*I Love Money*](/songs/i-love-money/) would pick up a year later. There's no bridge out; *we all got bills to pay* just stacks until it ends. The money track before *Big Hug* turned capitalism into a party hook.
