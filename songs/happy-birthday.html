Content

Track twelve on Masks and Monsters, feat. Gokudaxij (Eric Radloff; Apple Music and the official MV list Okudaxij). Co-written and performed with Luke Francis Walton; North Park lockdown, August 2020. The music video is Luke and Okudaxij on camera — HBD with Okudaxij on Scoobert’s channel (Jul 2020); the track also appears on the full visual album. Same collaborator as Adonde Eres on $WAMI$ and the features on Dragon Ball $d, here without Babidi and trading cartoon lore for a pandemic birthday.

It starts in lockdown solitude (no one there to celebrate / you can cry if you want), the same dissociated register as My Mind Is Slowly Slipping. Then the turn: you hear a knock / it's Amazon / open up / it's just your mom, package expectation becoming parent, and she pulls you onto the lawn for a drive-by parade, the 2020 substitute for a room full of people.

The sting is in the cleanup: no one left a single mess / not a shred of evidence / wish you were here. The spotless kindness turns into a Pink Floyd punchline. You miss the people who just left, or you miss normal life.

Less meme than Flip Flop Phil, more tender than Quarantine and Chill. The record's socially distanced celebration, before Coca Cola and Corona on the back half.
