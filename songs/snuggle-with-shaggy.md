Track two on [*Masks and Monsters*](/albums/masks-and-monsters/): Luke solo, North Park lockdown, August 2020. After [*It Can Get Worse*](/songs/it-can-get-worse/) opens on nightmare logic, this is the intimate flip. Same trap reframed as a honeymoon: the apartment as a desert island, the partner as the whole cast. *I could waste my life with you*, and time dilates to make that sound like a plan.

The jokes do the emotional work. Lockdown hobbies become courtship (cave paintings, going *all culinary*), COVID paranoia becomes a romance chore (*covering you when he coughs*), the supermarket run becomes an expedition in full PPE. Under it all sits a fact you can't argue with by repeating, *we are stuck no matter what we do*, with the relief right on top: waking up next to you makes you forget. The title is pun and cuddle command at once, the partner you hide under the blanket with.

Same register as [*Creature Comfort*](/songs/creature-comfort/) and [*Shaggy's Anthem*](/songs/shaggys-anthem/), but here the mask is domestic warmth, not bravado. Pairs with [*Pandemic Blues*](/songs/pandemic-blues/) as the other "I've got it good" lockdown song on the LP.
