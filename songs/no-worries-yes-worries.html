Content

Track fourteen on KŌAN, KŌAN C (June 30, 2022), immediately after instrumental Intro (track thirteen). Luke solo. KŌAN was built for headphones, with spatial and binaural work across the EP chapters; Intro is the clearest binaural example on the record, then this song lands. @beformer confirmed Indie Brandneu the day after KŌAN C dropped (July 1, 2022; next to Broken Bells) and Today's Indie Rock the next day; Nagamag corroborates both playlist adds. See Catalog: official playlists.

The title flips Don't Worry on Big Hug: not don't worry but no worries / yes worries at once, with no meaning and I'm running alongside. Verse one is mortality at a party, a shadow on my birthday and thinking about the permanent, then a bad bargain: a good deal on a Genie, came with a Monkey's Paw thrown in, every wish inverting. The immortality verse cuts off before naming hell, interrupted by an office verse that mirrors a song to quit your job to: boss wants us in the office, who am I to question, management as a shared delusion of agency that distracts from how little we're in control.

When forever comes back it finally names the destination, probably more like hell, a frozen-over hell, Dante's ice or just stuck forever bored, dissolving into oh wha ha ha ha ho, laughter as the only exit from the koan. Same wellness-command satire as High Society and Think About It, and part of the KŌAN C cluster with Who Am I Really Fooling Anyway and All My Friends Live on the Internet.
