Track four on [*Little Homes*](/albums/little-homes-fein/) (**May 31, 2016**), a breather after the opening trilogy of [*American Man*](/songs/american-man/), [*Sculptor*](/songs/sculptor/), and [*Girl You Can't Hide It*](/songs/girl-you-cant-hide-it/). Walton/Woodward co-write, with the album-wide [Frank Rosato](/with/frank-rosato/) mix. [Fresh Beats 365](https://freshbeats365.com/2016/03/10/fein-little-little-homes-ep-review/) (**Mar 10, 2016**, *Little Little Homes* teaser) called *Outro* the EP's *single most interesting soundscape* — vocals that *audibly dissolve from male to female*, passing through *twisted electronic voices* on the way.

The whole lyric: *couldn't find the time, but I don't mind, it suits me fine.* Three satire songs in about manhood, surgery, and a failing marriage, and the record stops to grant itself permission not to keep up. No time found, and that is fine. It leads into the viral single [*#Grownupz*](/songs/grownupz/). The LP uses these short cuts as palette cleansers between the heavier blocks.
