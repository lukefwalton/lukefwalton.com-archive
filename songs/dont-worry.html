Content

Track two on Big Hug (October 2021), out earlier as a standalone single (March 26, 2021, 91X Local Break). Luke solo. Wellness-app satire as a pop hook, where the title command fails on contact: don't worry, I'm gonna worry about it now. I'll worry that I'm not living in the now. "Living in the now" becomes one more thing to fail at.

The detail is what dates it to 2021. Panic gets penciled in (how does 2 pm sound?), the phone scolds you (Rock God, you haven't meditated in a week), and the angry Duolingo owl turns the language you're learning into the language you plead in. The bridge stacks an impossible self-care list, every wellness podcast at once, down to an 80 hour week, but make time for some fun. Then the song names its own mechanism: like when I say don't think of a cat, you think of a cat, which is ironic process theory in two lines. The outro lets the apps talk back in Japanese, meditate as a command and you want to live, right? as the darker punchline.

Same post-pandemic lane as I'm an Idiot and Scared to Reunite on the LP, lighter than Don't Know Much on Little Hug but the same spiral.

91X Local Break is also where a lot of the Encinitas origin lore lives on the record: Swami's, the Surfing Madonna, the Cardiff Kook, bandmates leaving for college, learning drums and bass by jamming alone. That feature was one of the first breaks that pulled Scoobert into wider attention and toward egoFM Munich. Full circle: Walton had interned at Surfdog Records in 2007–2008 on Brian Setzer and Dave Stewart releases, packing CDs into mailers while 91X played in the background.

91X kept spinning the project on Tim Pyles's Loudspeaker (2020–2024) and Planet Nü Nü, with documented playlist logs on 91x.com. @91xsandiego posted Hilary's Planet Nü Nü 91Xmas stack (Dec 24, 2023 broadcast) with Scoobert in the holiday lineup. Marquee placements: #1 Loudspeaker Top 5 for April 2021 and #90 on the Top 91 of 2021 countdown. In Munich, Don't Worry hit #1 on egoFM's ego 42 weekly chart (May 15, 2021, ↑ from #7) — the project's first #1 on a public weekly radio chart, after climbing ego 42 from #10 to #7 the week before. Walton visited the 91X studio with Tim Pyles in October 2021. Full spin index: /music/#music-industry (91X radio log).
