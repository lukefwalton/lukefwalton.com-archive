Content

Track seven on Little Homes (May 31, 2016), after Goodness Gracious. SoundCloud. Walton/Woodward co-write. It opens with a credited interlude, R.E.S.P.E.C.U.L8R (little bird) by Annie & Mae, which seeds the bird motif the song then turns cruel.

This is the darkest cut on the LP's first half. The chorus is a wish for self-erasure phrased as a favor: knock me out, swing the hammer, pull the trigger, leave no trace here of me now. Around it runs a sermon on hollow language: bury me in words if you think that I deserve it / imitate the sounds you've heard, and sing, you little bird, the listener reduced to a parrot. The bridge floods the little home outright, caved-in roof, water everywhere, creatures all in my hair, with chained up bodies in the back of the room and the hypocrite's creed do as I say but don't ya do as I do, before the whole thing buckles into a child's voice: save me pweez. The outro makes the point literal, talk itself as the weapon: I don't want you to elaborate, you're killing me.

Where BMI hears gun worship on American Man, here the trigger points inward. Before Intro (track eight); Twenty-Three follows.
