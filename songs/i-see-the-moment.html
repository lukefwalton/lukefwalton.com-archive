Content

Track thirteen on Big Hug, co-written and performed with J MESA (Taylor James / Babidi on earlier Scoobert records like Masks and Monsters and Dragon Ball $d; later Prisoner, 2024). Title split: Bandcamp lists I See the Monument; DistroKid and Apple Music list I See the Moment. Same song; this page uses the distributor title.

On a re-entry record turning toward strangers, this is the political-media hangover track. Belief keeps slipping out of reach: the book isn't clean, smeared in ink, meaning as a puzzle you can't read straight. The chorus puts the doubt next to its mirror image, people crying till they find the best way to our home against the cynical how much clout I'll need to get things started, belief versus the influence economy. Verse two names the source, white lies taking over the scene... beyond the popular feed, and ties it to breaking the spell sung by COVID disease. The bridge finally slows hope down and reaches recovery language, let's go home to a place that we know where all the good things go.

Heavier than Heffalumps and Woozles on the same LP, and the same Big Hug question as A Good Life on MMM: how to believe again after collapse. It leads toward KŌAN's disinformation thread (Miss Disinformation, to everyone who had a good pandemic).
