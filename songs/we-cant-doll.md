**KOMAGOME** is **YUUKA** and **CHIHIRO** (ex-[つぼみ大革命](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%81%A4%E3%81%BC%E3%81%BF%E5%A4%A7%E9%9D%A9%E5%91%BD)), [Rolling Stone Japan](https://rollingstonejapan.com/articles/detail/43491) and [ORICON](https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2403312/full/) frame *WE CAN'T DOLL* (**August 27, 2025**) as **MANA** and **KANA**'s **first produced artist after CHAI's breakup**: publicly legible as their first post-CHAI production lane, immediately before the [OKAME](/with/okame/) relaunch (September 2025). [B-PASS](https://b-pass.online/news/721): KOMAGOME asked MANA/KANA to help aim for a new **KAWAII**; the result is **underground pop** (punk, funk, pop fused).

[Uta-Net](https://www.uta-net.com/song/378731/) lists **週刊ナイナイミュージック** ending-theme placement, **作詞 MANA・KANA**, and **作曲 Scoobert Doobert・MANA・KANA** ([composer index](https://www.uta-net.com/composer/60093/) · [UtaTen](https://utaten.com/songWriter/4833365)); Apple Music credits Scoobert Doobert as **arranger and composer**. Luke Francis Walton **produced**. [Official MV](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rv94TTC_WM4) · [/music/#selected-videos](/music/#selected-videos). Follow-up: [CHAHHAN](/songs/chahhan/) (2026 · co-produced · mixed · performed). Walton mixed [OKAME *DEBUT*](/albums/okame-debut/) in the same MANA/KANA arc, the living Japanese pop graph, not a dead CHAI footnote.

No Scoobert lyrics. See [With KOMAGOME](/with/komagome/) · [With OKAME](/with/okame/) · [Catalog](/catalog/#production)
