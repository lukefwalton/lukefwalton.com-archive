Content

Track one on $WAMI$, the May 2018 lore-dump LP Luke Francis Walton cut in a bathrobe between Finding $D and Dragon Ball $d. Prometheus & Groove is the threshold: stolen-fire mythology in the title, funk pocket in the body, before Phone Plan’s wifi joke and the rest of the twenty-track collage.

On an album built from interludes, meme titles, and short sincere gestures, the opener signals that nothing here will behave like a conventional second album. Glasse Factory quotes Scoobert calling the era nostalgic wave; Bandcamp tags bedroom pop, lo-fi, alt-rock, San Diego. Prometheus & Groove is where that energy starts.
