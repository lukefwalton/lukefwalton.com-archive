Curator session version of [*see you again?*](/songs/see-you-again/), released **October 16, 2024** on **alexrainbirdRecords** as part of the **alexrainbirdSessions** series (*October 2024* playlist credit on Spotify). [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZDeYBgQkV90) hosts **Scoobert Doobert - see you again? | alexrainbirdSession**; [Deezer](https://www.deezer.com/us/track/3041740101) lists **See You Again? (alexrainbirdSessions)** with Luke Walton as lyricist on [alexrainbirdSessions October 2024](https://www.deezer.com/us/album/655465731). Same song DNA as the *I* album track and the pre-LP single: talisman in Kyoto, repainted dorm doors, the bridge cycling *maybe I will / I hope I will / I never will*.

The arrangement shift is the point. Spotify tags it **indie folk**: acoustic-forward, less coastal-travel production density than the album mix. Not a [*Live from the Void*](/albums/live-from-the-void/) virtual-concert cut; this is a third-party curator session for playlist discovery, in the lane of other stripped Scoobert placements (compare [alexrainbirdSessions](https://open.spotify.com/track/6KFO389ham8FH8lDrVaPIA) metadata vs. the [album version](https://open.spotify.com/album/2O9X06yNiwLSJbL8MLGC49)).

Parent song note unchanged: **for friends lost along the way**: drift, death, distance, not one breakup plot. The question mark in the title still refuses a clean answer.
