Track seven on [*Masks and Monsters*](/albums/masks-and-monsters/): Luke solo, North Park lockdown, August 2020. A one-joke interstitial after the emotional run of tracks one through six, in the same [memetic production](/albums/masks-and-monsters/) register the [Critical Meme Reader](/publications/masks-monsters-and-memes/) later named on this LP.

The lyrics are just the March 2020 public-health script, [CDC guidance](https://www.cdc.gov/clean-hands/about/index.html) to scrub for at least twenty seconds, turned into a chorus. The catch: the refrain runs exactly twenty seconds, so the song is a timer you can sing in the sink. Comedy that also works as instruction, same spirit as the album's cartoon shell carrying real COVID weight.

Sits between [*A Good Life*](/songs/a-good-life/) and the back-half absurdity, a palate cleanser between love-song denial and the rest of the monster tracklist. Pairs with [*Creature Comfort*](/songs/creature-comfort/) as another early-pandemic hygiene beat on the same record.
