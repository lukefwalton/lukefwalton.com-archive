Track eleven on [*Little Hug*](/albums/little-hug/) (**April 2021**), also track twelve on [*Big Hug*](/albums/big-hug/) as **Debby (Extended)** ([Apple](https://music.apple.com/us/album/debby-extended/1585121212?i=1585121225)): the same song and lyrics, with a guitar solo tacked onto the LP cut. Luke solo. Named grief before the title closer, [*A Little Hug*](/songs/a-little-hug/).

Two portraits and one plea. She's algorithm bait, *pink mullet*, *thirst trap*, *what a snack*; he's a *willow hanging on his phone / lying down before her throne*, worshipping someone who doesn't know he exists. The hook is the entire need, *Debby, please talk to me*, repeated and never answered. The second verse turns it into platform economics, a *blue check* and its cloud of simps, and the one moment that ever counted: *first comment at the start of it / back when she hearted it / lost in the scrolling pit*. A single heart emoji as peak intimacy, now buried in the feed.

Same internet loneliness as later [*High Society*](/songs/high-society/) and [*All My Friends Live on the Internet*](/songs/all-my-friends-live-on-the-internet/), but here it narrows to one person and one ask. After the *so what* spiral of [*Don't Know Much*](/songs/dont-know-much/), *Debby* is specific longing before the small comfort of the title track.
