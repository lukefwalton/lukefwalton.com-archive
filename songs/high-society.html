Content

Track twelve on KŌAN, KŌAN B era on the full LP (between More to Lose and instrumental Intro). Luke solo, same Riley Knapp master chain as the rest of the record. Satirical wellness pop with a party-invite panic underneath.

The opener names the trap and stutters on the anxious words: deleting all of my my social media, but then I'm never gonna get get get invited to another party. From there it longs for a decade it's too young to remember (party like it be 1999) and runs the good vibes only chorus as the culture's failed command, wellness language losing to the nervous system it forbids: no negativity, can't keep up with my anxiety. The high society hook chants class satire and the Instagram high life as one syllable, and verse two is option paralysis sliding into a confession, I'm a catfish, shallow in the deep end: the curated self as fraud, depth as performance.

The outro stacks I'm thinking about and momma make me happy until the delete-social-media verse comes back whole, the invite fear returning intact. Same KŌAN thread as All My Friends Live on the Internet and the LP-only disinformation pair (Miss Disinformation, to everyone who had a good pandemic). Lighter cousin of Don't Worry on Big Hug: same good vibes poison, more pop bounce.

Spotify editorial: @beformer confirmed Indie Brandneu (April 9, 2022); the same post thanks egoFM for on-air that week with host Max Klement — same April 2022 Munich radio thread as the Apr 6 interview promo. See Catalog: official playlists.
