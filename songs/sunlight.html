Content

Track three on MÖB, the deliberate turn toward warmth after Stories' head-loop. Ear to the Ground Music singled it out for bouncy funk energy, which fits its job: the album trying an answer to its own anxiety. The verses are a summer inventory (popsicle picnic, mango with Tajín, margarita number two) and the chorus is a prescription repeated until it sticks: imma be in the sunlight, throw a beer back, savor it.

The quietly load-bearing line is nobody needs me. That's the freedom move, not martyrdom but permission to exist without being on call. On an LP shadowed by illness and dread, Sunlight is the choice to step into the light anyway. It pairs with Tired of the Sunshine on Moonlight Beach as the push-pull on California brightness. Here the vote is for the sun.
