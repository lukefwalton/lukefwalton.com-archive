Content

Boomerang (with FEiN): February 1, 2016 single by Nina Francis and FEiN (Bandcamp). Written by Nina Francis and FEiN (Luke Francis Walton, Brandon Woodward); arranged, recorded, and produced by FEiN at Tiny Giant Recording; bass Brian Robert Jones; mixed/mastered Frank Rosato. USC orbit collab: San Diego Troubadour (Apr 2016): Francis and FEiN met at USC; local radio 91X and 94.9 spun the single.

Walton co-wrote Boomerang; did not write Between Dreams (2017 production only). The USC connection predates both: Neon Tommy on the April 2014 Thornton Pop Showcase at the Troubadour lists FEiN, Nina Francis, and Nick Belcher on the same senior lineup. Not on Little Homes; a FEiN collab alongside Marry You On the Dance Floor. With Nina Francis.

Long distance as physics. The verses run on departure arithmetic, the parenthetical countdowns (six hours til I see you, then nine once the flight slips) stretching the absence before it even begins. Home gets measured in visit length: this trip will only last a week or two.

The title turns that motion into an identity the narrator can't shake. Spun back and forth just like a boomerang, the want is plain and repeated (I wanna stay) but overruled by the return arc built into the trip. Opposite of Roadtrip (doesn't really matter anyway): here the narrator wants the little home to stick. Pairs with Out of My Mind (can't leave the ex in memory).
