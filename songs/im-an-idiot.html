Content

Breakout single and track six on Big Hug, pitched ahead of the album in September 2021 and added to Spotify New Music Friday around release (@beformer announcement, Sep 3, 2021). Luke solo. UNXIGNED read it as self-deprecation as freedom, not just confession, and that's the trick: the hook is a joke you can sing in public, with the hmm refrain doing the embarrassed punctuation.

The verses run a familiar tape. Faking the laugh and correcting mid-bit (ooo yeah, I mean no, I'm sorry dude, I didn't follow), envying the narcissists who shoot they shot and never think they miss, then lying awake while the brain keeps skipping over stupid things you've said. The bridge is a clean statement of the spotlight effect: nobody is really looking your way, you're only that important in your own head. The flip is generous, if anything you might be a little entertained by this idiot, the performance of idiocy turned into the actual product.

The outro widens it to everyone: no one cares 'cause we're all idiots. Same post-pandemic thesis as Don't Worry and Scared to Reunite, how to be a person again without pretending you aren't dumb or scared. Pairs with I Live in California and Heffalumps and Woozles, the first record that clearly reaches beyond the self-contained Scoobertverse (Big Hug essay).

CHAI spine: The New Music Friday placement is the documented hinge into CHAI hearing the song and commissioning Miracle (Scoobert Doobert Remix), then WHOLE, MY DREAM, and the broader Japan production run.
