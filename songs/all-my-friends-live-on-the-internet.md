Track twenty on [*KŌAN*](/albums/koan/), **KŌAN C** closer energy, also a standalone single with [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=16Xymn7W9_w).

The hook is a contradiction the song keeps on purpose: *all my friends live on the internet*, but *there's nothing virtual about you*. The relationships are real, the geography isn't. *Would you be mad if I said I never really wanna meet ya in-person* isn't antisocial; it's the fear that flesh would kill the vibe you already have. *You're probably my best friend* while *my mom thinks I'm pathetic / I think she's getting old*.

Then infrastructure as comedy: Wi-Fi down, DSP throttling, slap the router, neighbor's password is *Secret123*. Lucky me. A platonic love song for people who only exist as voice and memes and late-night co-op, and it treats that as worth celebrating. Pairs with [*5G BABY*](/songs/5g-baby/) (always-on pocket love) and [*4:20 pm*](/songs/4-20-pm/) (shared lonely rock). KŌAN C cluster with [*Who Am I Really Fooling Anyway*](/songs/who-am-i-really-fooling-anyway/), [無門関](/songs/mumonkan/), [*What Makes You You*](/songs/what-makes-you-you/).
