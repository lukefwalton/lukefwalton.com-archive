Content

FEiN single (2019), co-written by Luke Francis Walton and Brandon Michael Woodward. Mixed and mastered by Frank Rosato (same engineer as Little Homes). Not on the 2016 LP; later-era FEiN, same duo.

Instructions you can't follow, deafness to the script admitted upfront (tell me where to go / I'll never hear), jealousy run as a curated information diet (tell me when she hurts). The refrain is the line abusers and self-abandoners both use: please understand, this is better for me. Said twice, no irony tagged, which makes it scarier.

The chorus is the title contract, public devotion and private permission at once: behave and suffer only for you / then do whatever, do whatever when I leave the room. The second verse only deepens the submission, basic need filtered through control: lead me to the water though I only drink when she tells me no. FEiN theatrical pop treating a bad dynamic as a singalong, the Little Homes social-satire muscle applied to intimacy instead of suburbia. Pairs with 800,000 (apocalyptic love exit) as the other 2019 FEiN single on this site.
