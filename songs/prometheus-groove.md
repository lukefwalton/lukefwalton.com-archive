Track one on [*$WAMI$*](/albums/wami/), the May 2018 lore-dump LP Luke Francis Walton cut in a bathrobe between [*Finding $D*](/albums/finding-d-remastered/) and [*Dragon Ball $d*](/albums/dragon-ball-d/). **Prometheus & Groove** is the threshold: stolen-fire mythology in the title, funk pocket in the body, before [*Phone Plan*](/songs/phone-plan/)’s wifi joke and the rest of the twenty-track collage.

On an album built from interludes, meme titles, and short sincere gestures, the opener signals that nothing here will behave like a conventional second album. [Glasse Factory](https://glassefactory.com/scoobert-dooberts-new-track-think-about-it-is-experimental-funk-at-its-finest/) quotes Scoobert calling the era nostalgic wave; Bandcamp tags bedroom pop, lo-fi, alt-rock, San Diego. **Prometheus & Groove** is where that energy starts.
