Content

Track five on Finding $D, Luke solo; every song on the LP was written, recorded, mixed, and mastered in a single day. It sits between My Meddling Kids (Hallelujah Sexy) and Economic Kama Sutra, and it treats anxiety as religion.

The intro calls for help in three directions, mother, brother, and a hey oh Jesus, stop where stop is both prayer and plea. Then the pre-chorus names the real deity: anxiety, addressed as my oldest love, OG, the longest relationship in the room.

From there the chorus takes the idiom and runs it until it breaks (damned if you do if you did if you did), with no exit branch left. A brief riot offers collective violence as a release valve before the loop closes back over it. The ending says afraid of missing out twice: FOMO as the decision the song never manages to make. Same dread register as My Friend, Scoobert and, later, Take a Breath (body panic on Little Hug), except the OG here is anxiety, not COVID.
