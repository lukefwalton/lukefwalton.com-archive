Content

FEiN single (June 20, 2019), co-written by Luke Francis Walton and Brandon Michael Woodward. Mixed by Cary Singer, mastered by Riley Knapp, the same engineering chain as 800,000. Not on Little Homes (2016); later-era FEiN, same duo.

Grief that won't drain. The opening sits underwater (settled in the sediment), then the chorus runs through bargains that never land: money, a letter, learning to love the wound. None of it moves the fact underneath, I'm not getting over you.

The title does the work. A culling is selective death, what gets removed when you can't let go, and forever asleep with you turns romance into burial. The last chorus can't even finish its sentence, trailing off on I'm not getting over. No suburb satire here, just a locked room and a stack of refrains. Pairs with 800,000 (apocalyptic exit) and Behave (control dynamics).
