Track twenty-two on [*KŌAN*](/albums/koan/), **LP-only** (fourth movement with *September*, *1101*, [*4:20 pm*](/songs/4-20-pm/), [*Miss Disinformation*](/songs/miss-disinformation/), [*to everyone who had a good pandemic*](/songs/to-everyone-who-had-a-good-pandemic/)). Not on the KŌAN A/B/C chapters. Luke solo. [@beformer announced official Spotify Soirée placement](https://www.instagram.com/p/ChNXXzZJ8Qh/) **August 13, 2022**, ahead of the full LP (**September 9, 2022**). The drums come from a **cleared Clyde Stubblefield sample pack**, James Brown's *Funky Drummer* lineage made literal, while the lyric uses *funk* to mean the bad thought stuck on loop.

The chorus is obligation pop: depression as an earworm (*do what I got to do / for me to get through*) that you serve someone else through anyway. The verse is couple mismatch after lockdown, *you want to go out / I want to stay in*, social energy against collapse, the same tension as [*Creature Comfort*](/songs/creature-comfort/) in relationship pronouns.

Two bridges supply the funk. One is notification dread, the good morning ruined by a *New York Times notification* and *getting kind of used to apocalyptic situations*, *living through history / shot in 4k*, the same media vertigo as [*I See the Moment*](/songs/i-see-the-moment/) on *Big Hug*. The other is wage-labor mental health: *hourly employees like me don't get mental health days*, so *buy more coffee / it helps with anxiety*. No HR, just caffeine. The outro doubles *down down down* and lets the funk win the last bar. Pairs with [*No Worries, Yes Worries*](/songs/no-worries-yes-worries/) (title-track anxiety flip) and the KŌAN C internet cluster.
